================================================================================
ASSUMPTIONS FOR BACKTESTING SOLUTION
================================================================================

This document lists all assumptions made in the backtesting implementation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. TRADING CONSTRAINTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 Sequential Trading
    - Only ONE position can be open at a time
    - Must close current position before opening a new one
    - Interpretation: "a sequence of trades" = sequential, not parallel

1.2 Full Capital Deployment
    - Each trade uses 100% of available balance
    - No partial position sizing

1.3 Single Day Evaluation
    - Each day is evaluated independently
    - Starting balance: $10,000 fresh each day
    - No carry-over of balance between days

1.4 Trade Execution
    - Trades execute at the 'close' price of each 1-minute candle
    - Instant execution assumed (no slippage)
    - Can enter and exit at the same minute (on different tickers)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. MARKET CONDITIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 No Trading Fees
    - Zero commission on all trades
    - No spread costs

2.2 Unlimited Liquidity
    - Can buy/sell any amount at the quoted price
    - No market impact

2.3 Perfect Knowledge
    - Full knowledge of all future prices within the day
    - Prices are known from minute 0 to minute 1439

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. POSITION TYPES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 Long Positions
    - Buy asset, profit when price increases
    - Profit = quantity × (exit_price - entry_price)

3.2 Short Positions
    - Sell asset (borrow), profit when price decreases
    - Profit = quantity × (entry_price - exit_price)
    - No borrowing costs or margin requirements

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. DATA INTERPRETATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 Price Data
    - Using 1-minute Binance futures klines
    - 'close' price used for all calculations
    - Data covers December 1-31, 2025

4.2 Available Tickers
    - BTCUSDT (Bitcoin)
    - ETHUSDT (Ethereum)
    - BNBUSDT (Binance Coin)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. TARGET INTERPRETATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 $1M Target
    - Goal: Determine if $10,000 → $1,000,000 is achievable
    - Evaluation period: Single 24-hour day
    - If achievable: Print the sequence of trades

5.2 Maximum Profit
    - Secondary goal: Find maximum achievable profit
    - Report the optimal trade sequence regardless of $1M achievement

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. ALGORITHM CHOICE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 Dynamic Programming Selected
    - Finds globally optimal solution
    - Better than greedy approach (16x improvement observed)
    - Trade-off: Higher computational cost O(N² × M)

6.2 Why Not Greedy?
    - Greedy picks best immediate trade
    - Misses compounding opportunities from shorter trades
    - Example: 10 trades at 3% each = 34% total > single 10% trade

================================================================================
